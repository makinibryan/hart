H-art Of Africa Mt. Longonot Hike Experience!

Do you remember how long those nights before a school trip were? That's exactly how mine was. I was super pumped for this one because hiking is one of the ways I get my adrenaline going.

So, morning FINALLY came, I met up with the rest of my squad for the day at around 6:30am. We were clearly all in high spirits and ready to take on the day.

After that, we setback on our journey to Mt. Longonot. We got there safely and were quick to be updated on the new park guidelines on plastic water bottles and a fact sheet on the hike difficulty, estimated time and history of Rift Valleyâ€™s formation by our guide!

We embarked the hike and I can tell you for sure, the energy we started with went fading! haha. Slowly realizing that the lockdown had kept most of us unfit, we wrestled with the escalation and rocky ascend to the crater!

Some of us got muscle pulls along the way, others gave up and sat but the good news is we all got to the top. Some managed to go round the crater and made it to the back with smiles of achievement! We were extremely proud of ourselves.

We really got caught up in the moment celebrating that we made it to the top and round the crater, taking photos and discussing how tired we were not remembering we had another 3.1kms to cover back down. Part of me was still excited but part of me really wondered if I'd make it with the descent especially being so steep!

How many of you think that going down a hill is easier than going up? Well, the good news is that we all made it.

We all assembled in the buses and went to Zambezi Lodge for what was meant to be lunch. Let me tell you, that lunch was hard earned!

There, we danced quite a bit in celebration of our great achievements and a successful day.

We then celebrated our October babies with a delightful cake then drove back to Nairobi.
