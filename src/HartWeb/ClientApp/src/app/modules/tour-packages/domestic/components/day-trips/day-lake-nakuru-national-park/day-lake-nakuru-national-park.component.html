Lake Nakuru National Park

Leave your Nairobi hotel, Residence or apartment with our safari driver guide between 0600 am and 0700 am and driver via Waiyaki Way with a stop over at the view point where you will see the escarpment wall. Escarpment is the wall that was left after the formation of the great Rift Valley, enjoy amazing views and take great photos. Descend the escarpment wall towards Naivasha with a view of Mount Longonot and lake Naivasha from the main highway.

You will get to see animals such as Zebras, Giraffes and Gazelles that graze alongside the Nairobi – Nakuru highway immediately after passing Naivasha town. Proceed arriving Nakuru town before 1000 am, pick the picnic lunch and drive to the main entrance gate. Have a great game drive as you watch the black and white rhinos, Flamingos and other wildlife that inhabit in this National Park.

Lake Nakuru is Kenya’s most visited National Park. Located 160 km and 2½ hours from Nairobi, the park is an important Ramsar site and home to thousands of different bird species.

The 188 sq km Lake Nakuru is the only KWS-administered reserve that is fully enclosed. Once just a bird sanctuary, Lake Nakuru is now the best place in Kenya to see the highly protected black and white rhino. Among other animals found in the park include rhino, lion, leopard, flamingos, Egyptian geese, waterbuck, giraffe, hyena, buffalo, baboons, vervet monkeys, gazelles. Enjoy your picnic lunch inside the park and continue with the game drive upto 1500 hrs or 1530hrs, check out and drive back to Nairobi.

On the way back you can make a stopover at Lake Naivasha where an optional activity such as boat ride can be arranged at extra cost for bird watching and Hippopotamus. Later with your guide drive direct to Nairobi where you will be dropped at your Nairobi Hotel or Residence.
